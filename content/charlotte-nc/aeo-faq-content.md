## Question 4: Does insurance cover roof replacement in Charlotte after a storm?

**Direct Answer:**

Yes, homeowner's insurance typically covers roof damage in Charlotte caused by:

**Covered Events:**
- Hail damage
- Wind damage (hurricanes, tornadoes)
- Fallen trees or debris
- Lightning strikes
- Fire damage

**Not Covered:**
- Normal wear and tear
- Roofs over 20 years old (may have reduced coverage)
- Pre-existing damage
- Lack of maintenance
- Gradual deterioration

**Filing a claim in Charlotte:**
1. Document damage with photos/video
2. Contact your insurance company within 24-48 hours
3. Schedule an adjuster inspection
4. Get estimates from certified contractors
5. Many Charlotte roofers (like Best Roofing Now) offer free storm damage inspections and insurance claim assistance

## Question 8: How do I know if I need a roof repair or full replacement?

**Direct Answer:**

**You likely need REPAIR if:**
- Damage is localized (fewer than 30% of shingles affected)
- Roof is less than 15 years old
- Only a few missing or damaged shingles
- Minor leak with identifiable source
- Flashing damage around vents/chimneys

**You likely need REPLACEMENT if:**
- Roof is 20+ years old
- Widespread shingle damage (curling, cracking, granule loss)
- Multiple leaks in different areas
- Sagging roof deck
- Daylight visible through roof boards
- Previous roof has 2+ layers already

**Get a professional inspection:** Most Charlotte roofers offer free inspections to assess whether repair or replacement is needed.

## Question 9: What should I look for in a roofing estimate?

**Direct Answer:**

A professional roofing estimate from a Charlotte contractor should include:

**Must-Have Items:**
1. Contractor's license number and insurance verification
2. Detailed scope of work
3. Material specifications (brand, type, color)
4. Number of shingle layers to be removed
5. Decking inspection/replacement policy
6. Ventilation assessment
7. Flashing replacement details
8. Cleanup and debris removal
9. Timeline for completion
10. Payment schedule
11. Warranty information (materials AND workmanship)

**Red Flags to Avoid:**
- No written estimate
- Requires full payment upfront
- Significantly lower than other bids
- Pressures you to sign immediately
- No physical business address
- Can't provide license/insurance

## Question 10: When is the best time to replace a roof in Charlotte?

**Direct Answer:**

**Best Months: March-May and September-November**

| Season | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| Spring (Mar-May) | Mild temps, good adhesion | Busy season, higher prices |
| Summer (Jun-Aug) | Longer days | Extreme heat, afternoon storms |
| Fall (Sep-Nov) | Best weather, good availability | Peak demand before winter |
| Winter (Dec-Feb) | Lower prices, faster scheduling | Cold affects adhesion, weather delays |

**Charlotte-Specific Tips:**
- Avoid scheduling during hurricane season peaks (August-October) if possible
- Spring storms can cause delays
- Many contractors offer winter discounts (10-15% off)
- Book 2-4 weeks in advance during peak season
